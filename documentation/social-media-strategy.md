# FootyScope — Social Media Marketing Strategy

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Platforms:** Instagram (primary), X/Twitter (secondary), TikTok (growth), Facebook (syndication)

---

## 1. Brand Positioning

**Voice:** Analytical, direct, opinionated — not hype-driven. FootyScope is the "smart friend who actually watches the kids play" rather than a clickbait aggregator.

**Tagline options:**
- "See the draft differently."
- "Every prospect. Every angle."
- "The sharpest eye on the draft."

**Visual identity on social:** Dark mode aesthetic matching the site. Bold typography, clean stat graphics, radar charts as a signature visual element. The Scope Rating becomes instantly recognisable branding.

---

## 2. Platform Strategy

### Instagram (Primary — @footyscope)

Instagram is where AFL draft culture lives. @thedraftscout, Baseline Sports, and Rookie Me Central are all active here. This is where you build the brand.

**Content pillars:**

| Pillar | Format | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Prospect Spotlights** | Carousel (5-7 slides) | 3x/week | Drive traffic to site profiles |
| **Scope Ratings Reveal** | Single image or Reel | 1-2x/week | Build the rating system as a brand |
| **Hot Takes / Opinions** | Text-on-image or Stories | Daily | Engagement + personality |
| **Matchday Recaps** | Carousel or Reel | After key CTL rounds | Timeliness + authority |
| **Rankings Movement** | Carousel (risers/fallers) | Fortnightly | Recurring series people follow |
| **Draft Class Comparisons** | Side-by-side graphic | 1x/week | Shareable, debate-starting |
| **Behind the Scope** | Stories/Reels | 1-2x/week | Personal brand, process, credibility |

**Carousel template — Prospect Spotlight (5 slides):**
1. Player photo + name + Scope Rating badge
2. Radar chart (the 9 attributes)
3. Key stats (3-4 standout numbers, bold typography)
4. Scouting verdict (2-3 sentences — strengths, weaknesses, projection)
5. CTA: "Full profile on FootyScope.com.au" + link in bio prompt

**Reels ideas:**
- "60-second scout" — rapid-fire player breakdown over highlights
- "Overrated or underrated?" — hot take format
- "Draft stock check" — weekly risers and fallers
- "Who's the better prospect?" — A vs B debate starter
- Time-lapse of building a player profile on the site (behind the scenes)

**Stories strategy:**
- Polls: "Where does [Player] go? Pick 1-5 / 6-12 / 13+"
- Quizzes: "Which prospect has the highest Scope Rating?"
- Q&A boxes before big games or events
- Countdown stickers for Combine, National Champs, Draft Night
- Share every new article/profile with a swipe-up or link sticker

### X / Twitter (Secondary — @footyscope)

X is where AFL media and draft analysts live. It's less visual but better for real-time discussion, networking with industry people, and SEO-adjacent link sharing.

**Content approach:**
- Share every new site article/profile with a key insight pulled out as the tweet text
- Live-tweet during CTL games, Combine, and Draft Night
- Quote-tweet and engage with Cal Twomey, Knightmare, Wolfman, and other draft analysts
- Thread format for "Top 10 breakdown" or "Draft class deep dive"
- Contrarian takes get engagement — have an opinion and back it with data

**Posting cadence:** 1-2 tweets/day minimum, more during key events.

**Networking priority:** Follow and engage with AFL journalists, draft analysts, player managers, and club recruiters. Reply thoughtfully to their content. This is how you get on their radar.

---

### TikTok (Growth — @footyscope)

TikTok is underutilised in AFL draft content. Most creators there are highlights-only. There's a gap for analytical, personality-driven draft content.

**Content approach:**
- Repurpose Instagram Reels (adjust aspect ratio/captions as needed)
- "Draft stock" series — 30-60 second hot takes
- "Scout with me" — sit down with a laptop, break down game tape
- React to other draft predictions or mock drafts
- Trending audio + AFL draft overlay (when it works naturally, don't force it)

**Posting cadence:** 3-5x/week, repurposed from Instagram Reels with TikTok-native captions.

---

### Facebook (Syndication)

Low effort, high reach for older AFL demographic. Primarily a syndication channel.

- Auto-post every article from the site
- Share Instagram carousels as Facebook posts
- Join and contribute to AFL draft Facebook groups (BigFooty-adjacent communities)
- Facebook is where parents of prospects and older fans hang out — good for the paid scouting reports funnel

## 4. Growth Tactics

### Phase 1 — Pre-Launch & Launch (Weeks 1-4)

**Goal:** 500 followers on Instagram, establish brand presence.

- **Teaser campaign:** Post 5-7 "coming soon" graphics with the FootyScope branding. Tease the Scope Rating system, show radar chart previews, hint at the rankings page.
- **Launch week blitz:** Post daily for 7 days straight. Alternate between prospect spotlights, the methodology explainer, and hot takes.
- **DM outreach:** Message 20-30 AFL draft fan accounts. Not spammy — genuine "hey, I'm launching a draft scouting site, would love your feedback" approach.
- **Hashtag strategy:** #AFLDraft #AFLDraft2026 #AFL #CoatesTalentLeague #AFLProspects #DraftScouting — use 5-10 relevant hashtags per post, mix broad and niche.
- **Cross-promote:** Link Instagram from the site footer, about page, and every article. Link the site from Instagram bio.

### Phase 2 — Build Authority (Months 2-3)

**Goal:** 1,000-2,000 followers, become a recognised voice.

- **Collaborate:** Reach out to @thedraftscout, smaller draft accounts for shoutouts or joint content (e.g., "we each rank our top 5 mids and compare").
- **Event-driven content:** National Championships and Combine are content goldmines. Post heavily around these events — live reactions, instant player assessments, updated rankings.
- **Engage relentlessly:** Reply to every comment on your posts. Comment on competitor posts with thoughtful takes (not self-promotion). Be part of the conversation.
- **User-generated content:** "Drop your mock top 5 in the comments" — engagement bait that actually works in draft communities.
- **Weekly recurring series:** People follow for series. "Scope Rating Reveal Tuesday" and "Risers & Fallers Sunday" become appointment content.

### Phase 3 — Pre-Draft Surge (Months 4-6, leading into November draft)

**Goal:** 3,000-5,000 followers, drive significant site traffic.

- **Phantom Draft series:** Release your full phantom draft as a multi-part carousel series (picks 1-10, 11-20, etc.). This is the single highest-engagement content type in AFL draft media.
- **Draft countdown content:** Daily prospect features in the final 30 days before the draft.
- **Draft Night live coverage:** Live Stories, live tweets, instant reactions to every pick. This is your "Super Bowl" — go all in.
- **Post-draft grades:** Immediate club-by-club draft grades. Fast > perfect here. First-mover advantage is huge.
- **Email capture:** Use the draft hype period to push newsletter signups or EOI form submissions for paid scouting reports.

## 5. Engagement Strategy

### Community Building
- **Reply to every comment** in the first 6 months. Every single one.
- **Ask questions in captions:** "Where do you have [Player] on your board?" / "Agree or disagree?"
- **Reshare fan takes** to Stories with your response — makes followers feel seen.
- **Create debate:** Deliberately post takes you know will split opinion. "Player X is the most overrated prospect in the class" drives comments.

### Networking with Industry
- Follow and engage with: Cal Twomey, Knightmare, Wolfman, Baseline Sports, Rookie Me Central, club recruiters with public accounts
- Quote-tweet their content with your own analysis — adds value, gets you noticed
- Attend Combine / National Champs if possible and post content from there (in-person content >> remote analysis for credibility)

### Driving Site Traffic
- Every Instagram post should reference the full profile/article on the site
- Use link stickers in Stories (not just "link in bio")
- Pin a "Start here" post or Reel that explains what FootyScope is and links to the site
- Track which social content drives the most site visits (UTM parameters on links)

## 6. Paid Scouting Reports — Social Funnel

The EOI form for paid junior scouting reports needs a soft, trust-building funnel:

1. **Free content builds credibility** — your prospect analysis proves you know what you're talking about
2. **Stories/posts mention the service casually** — "I also do individual scouting reports for families — DM me or check the link in bio"
3. **Testimonials** — once you've done a few, share anonymised feedback (with permission)
4. **Seasonal push** — promote harder around draft combine time when families are most anxious about their kid's prospects
5. **Don't hard-sell** — the content IS the pitch. If your analysis is good, people will come to you.
